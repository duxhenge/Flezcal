# Flezcal User Manual

## What is Flezcal?

Flezcal is a community-powered guide for finding specific foods and drinks you're passionate about. The name combines "flan" and "mezcal" -- two of the original categories -- but the concept extends far beyond those two items. A "Flezcal" is any specific food or drink you love and want to track down wherever you go.

The app helps you answer one question: **"Does this place have what I'm looking for?"**

Rather than reviewing entire restaurants, Flezcal focuses on individual items. You might want to know which bars near you carry mezcal, which bakeries make flan, or which restaurants serve wood-fired pizza. Flezcal finds those places, checks their websites automatically, and lets the community verify and rate them.

## Setting Expectations: Web Checks vs. Community Verification

Flezcal uses two systems to help you find spots, and it's important to understand what each one does.

**Web checks** are automated scans that happen when you tap on a suggested pin. The app visits the venue's website and searches for keywords related to your picks. This is a best-effort scan -- it works well when restaurants list their menu items online, but it can miss items that aren't on the website, and occasionally it may match a keyword out of context. The app tells you upfront: "Web searches aren't as reliable as your own knowledge -- but we'll give it our best shot!"

**Community verification** is powered by real people. When a user has physically been to a spot and confirms it serves a particular item, that carries more weight than any automated scan. Users can vote thumbs-up or thumbs-down on each category at a spot, building a trust score over time.

Think of web checks as a helpful first guess, and community verification as the real answer. Both work together -- the web check helps you decide whether a place is worth visiting, and your verification after visiting helps everyone else.

## Getting Started

### Age Verification

The first time you open Flezcal, you'll see an age verification screen asking "Are you 21 or older?" Because the app includes alcohol-related categories (mezcal, bourbon, scotch, etc.), you must confirm you're 21+ to proceed. Tap **"Yes, I'm 21+"** to continue. This only appears once.

### Welcome Screen

After age verification, a welcome screen introduces the app's key features:

- Discover Flezcals on an interactive map
- Add your favorite spots
- Rate your finds
- Climb the leaderboard as you contribute
- Ghost pins show unconfirmed spots -- help verify them

Tap **"Let's go!"** to enter the app. This screen may reappear when new features are added -- you can also revisit it anytime from **Profile > What's New**.

### Signing In

You can browse the map and explore without signing in, but you'll need an account to add spots, rate them, or verify categories. Flezcal offers two sign-in options:

- **Sign in with Apple** -- the fastest option, uses your Apple ID
- **Continue with Email** -- create an account with your email and a password

You can sign in from the **Profile** tab or when you try to add a spot.

## Your Picks: Choosing What You're Looking For

Your picks are the heart of the app. They determine what shows up on your map, which ghost pins appear, and what the web checker searches for. You can have up to **3 picks** at a time -- any combination of built-in and custom categories.

### Viewing and Managing Picks

Go to the **My Flezcals** tab (heart icon) to see your current picks. New users start with three defaults: Mezcal, Flan, and Handmade Tortillas.

Each pick card shows:
- The category emoji and name
- The first few search terms used to find it on websites
- An **edit button** (slider icon) to customize search terms
- A **remove button** (X icon) to remove it from your picks

Below your active picks, empty slots show how many more you can add. Tap an empty slot or the **"Customize My Flezcals"** button to open the full category grid.

### Available Categories

Flezcal launches with 23 curated categories across several groups:

**The Launch Trio:** Mezcal, Flan, Handmade Tortillas

**Drinks:** Bourbon, Fernet Branca, New England IPA, Single Malt Scotch

**Savory:** Peameal Bacon, Wood-Fired Pizza, Paella, Oysters, Pho, Pozole, Tartare, Fugu, Bibimbap, Iberico Ham, Caviar, Pierogi, Lobster Rolls, Smashburgers

**Sweets & Specialty:** Maple Syrup, Artisan Chocolate

To add or remove a category, tap it in the grid. Selected categories show a checkmark. A counter at the top shows how many slots you've used.

### Creating a Custom Category

Can't find what you're looking for? Tap **"Create Your Own"** at the bottom of the category grid. Custom categories count toward your 3-pick limit, so you'll need to remove an existing pick if all slots are full.

1. **Name your category** -- be specific. "Pupusas" or "Empanadas" works great. Broad terms like "Italian" or "Seafood" are too generic and will be rejected.
2. **Choose an emoji** from the provided selection.
3. **Review the search terms** -- the app auto-generates terms based on your category name. You can add, remove, or modify these (see below).
4. Tap **"Create & Add to My Flezcals"** to add it to your picks.

Custom categories appear with a purple color scheme to distinguish them from built-in categories.

### Adjusting Web Search Terms

Every category has a set of search terms that the app uses when scanning venue websites. You can customize these for any pick -- built-in or custom.

1. Go to **My Flezcals** tab
2. Tap the **edit button** (slider icon) on any pick card
3. The **Edit Search Terms** screen shows your current terms as removable chips

**To remove a term:** Tap the X on any chip.

**To add a term:** Type a new term in the text field and tap the + button. Think about words that restaurants actually use on their menus. For example, if you're looking for mezcal, you might add terms like "agave spirits" or "mezcaleria."

**To reset:** Tap **"Reset to defaults"** to restore the original search terms.

Tap **"Save Changes"** when done. Future website scans will use your updated terms.

**Tips for effective search terms:**
- Use words that appear on actual restaurant menus and websites
- Include common alternate spellings (e.g., "pierogi" and "pierogies")
- Include terms in other languages if relevant (e.g., "flan casero" for flan)
- Avoid overly common words that would match unrelated content

## The Map (Explore Tab)

The **Explore** tab (map icon) is the main screen. It shows three types of markers:

### Your Location

A standard blue dot shows where you are.

### Confirmed Spots (Solid Pins)

These are venues that community members have added to Flezcal. They have solid, colored pins with the category emoji. Tap one to see full details, ratings, and offerings.

### Ghost Pins (Suggested Spots)

Ghost pins are unconfirmed suggestions pulled from Apple Maps. They represent places that *might* have what you're looking for but haven't been verified yet.

**Yellow ghost pins** (dashed border with "?" icon) are untested suggestions. The app hasn't checked their website yet.

**Green ghost pins** (solid border with checkmark icon, gently pulsing) are likely matches. During a batch pre-screen, the app did a quick homepage scan and found keywords related to one of your picks. These are worth checking first.

### Using the Map

**Filter by category:** Tap a pick name in the filter bar at the top to show only that category. Tap "All" to show everything.

**Search a new area:** Pan or zoom the map, then tap the **"Search This Area"** button that appears at the bottom. The app will fetch new ghost pin suggestions and run a quick pre-screen of their homepages.

**Center on your location:** Tap the location button (top right) to snap back to where you are.

**Refresh spots:** Tap the refresh button (top right) to reload confirmed spots from the database.

### Tapping a Ghost Pin

When you tap any ghost pin, the map centers it and a detail sheet slides up. Here's what happens:

1. The app identifies the venue's website (from Apple Maps data)
2. It runs an automated **3-pass website check**:
   - **Pass 1:** Downloads the venue's homepage and menu subpages, scanning for your pick's keywords
   - **Pass 2:** If Pass 1 didn't find a match, searches the web for your keywords on that venue's domain
   - **Pass 3:** (Only for venues with no website) Searches the broader web for your pick at that venue name
3. While the primary pick is being checked, all your other picks are scanned against the same cached web pages at no extra cost

The sheet shows:
- **Category chips** indicating what was found (colored checkmark), what had a partial match (amber question mark), and what wasn't found (gray)
- A **result banner** explaining what the web check found
- A **disclaimer** reminding you that web results aren't perfect

From this sheet you can:
- **"Yes, add it to Flezcal!"** -- if you know this place has what you're looking for
- **"View in Apple Maps"** -- open the venue in Apple Maps for directions
- **"Not accurate, remove pin"** -- dismiss the ghost pin if it's not relevant

## Adding a Spot

There are two ways to add a spot to Flezcal:

### From a Ghost Pin

Tap a ghost pin on the map, then tap **"Yes, add it to Flezcal!"** This pre-fills the venue information and any categories the web check confirmed.

### From Explore Search

1. Go to the **Spots** tab and switch to **Explore** mode
2. Search for a venue by name
3. Tap any search result to open the detail sheet
4. Tap **"Yes, add it to Flezcal!"** to add it

### The Confirm Spot Screen

After selecting a venue, the Confirm Spot screen shows:
- A map preview with the venue's location
- The venue name and address, verified via Apple Maps
- If the spot already exists on Flezcal, a banner tells you -- your additions will merge with the existing entry

**Adding Offerings:**

Each category has a specific type of offering you can list. For example:
- **Mezcal** asks for "Mezcal Brands" (e.g., Del Maguey, Vago, Bozal)
- **Flan** asks for "Flan Styles" (e.g., Classic, Coconut, Cheese Flan)
- **Oysters** asks for "Oyster Varieties" (e.g., Wellfleet, Kumamoto, Blue Point)
- **Wood-Fired Pizza** asks for "Pizza Styles" (e.g., Margherita, Marinara, Diavola)

Enter one item per field. Tap **"Add Another"** to list more. Offerings are optional but helpful -- they let other users know exactly what's available.

Tap **"Add This Spot"** (or **"Update This Spot"** if it already exists) to save.

**First spot celebration:** When you add your very first spot, you'll get a congratulatory message: "¡Eres un Flanático!" Welcome to the community.

## Spot Details

Tap any confirmed spot on the map or in the Spots list to see its full detail page.

### What You'll See

- **Photo:** A map snapshot showing the venue's location.
- **Category badges:** Colored chips showing all categories this spot is tagged with (e.g., Mezcal, Flan).
- **Name and address**
- **Average rating:** Displayed as a flan emoji bar (instead of stars) with a numeric average and the number of ratings.
- **Fun badges:** "Hidden Gem" (for highly-rated spots with few ratings) or "New" (added within the last 30 days).

### Verification Section

Under "Help verify this spot," each category has thumbs-up and thumbs-down buttons. If you've been to this place and can confirm whether they serve a particular item, vote! A confirmation percentage shows the community consensus (e.g., "85% of users confirm Mezcal").

### Offerings

Listed by category. For example, a mezcal spot might show brands like "Del Maguey," "Vago," and "Bozal." You can add more offerings by tapping the + button if the category isn't locked by the owner.

### Adding Another Category

If you know a spot serves something that isn't listed yet, tap **"Add Another Category"** at the bottom. This runs a website check for the new category and lets you add it to the spot.

### Actions

- **Open in Maps:** Get directions via Apple Maps
- **Rate:** Give your rating (see Rating section below)
- **Report Spot:** Flag inaccurate or inappropriate listings
- **Report as Permanently Closed:** Let the community know if a place has shut down

## Rating a Spot

Flezcal uses a flan-based rating scale instead of stars:

| Rating | Label | Meaning |
|--------|-------|---------|
| 1 flan | Meh | Wouldn't order it again |
| 2 flans | Decent | Fine, nothing special |
| 3 flans | Legit | Solid, the real deal |
| 4 flans | Fire | One of the best I've had |
| 5 flans | Obsessed | Best I've ever had, I'm obsessed |

To rate a spot:
1. Open the spot detail page
2. Tap the **"Rate"** button
3. If the spot has multiple categories, select which one you're rating
4. Choose your rating level
5. Submit

Only rate items you've personally tried at that location.

## The Spots Tab (List View)

The **Spots** tab (list icon) has two modes, toggled by a segmented control at the top:

### Community Mode

Shows all confirmed Flezcal spots from the database. Use the filter pills to narrow by category. Each row shows:
- Category icons
- Spot name
- Status (rating bar, verification status, or "New")
- Distance from your location in miles

### Explore Mode

A live search powered by Apple Maps. Type a venue name to find any restaurant, bar, or store -- even ones not yet on Flezcal.

Results show:
- An icon indicating the venue type (fork/knife for restaurants, wineglass for bars, mug for breweries, etc.)
- Green highlight if the homepage pre-screen found a keyword match
- Venue name, address, and distance

Tap any result to open the same ghost pin detail sheet, complete with an automated website check. From there you can add it to Flezcal or tap **"Show on Map"** to see it on the map.

**Searching other cities:** You can search for venues in any location, not just near you.

## For Venue Owners

If you own or manage a spot that's on Flezcal, you can update your listing just like any other user -- for free. To go further, contact support@flezcal.app to become an **Owner Verified** spot. Owner verification gives you:

- A verified badge on your listing
- The ability to lock your menu details so others can't modify them
- A section to list your featured brands
- A reservation link on your spot page

## Tips and Tricks

- **Shake your phone** for a surprise.
- **Green ghost pins are your best bet** -- the app already found keyword matches on their website. Tap those first.
- **Edit your search terms** if you're not getting good results. Adding terms that restaurants actually use on their menus makes the web check more accurate.
- **The web check scans menu subpages too**, not just the homepage. It follows links to pages like "/menu" or "/food" to find your items.
- **Your picks shape everything.** Change your picks and the map, ghost pins, and filters all update to match.
- **You can search other cities** in the Explore tab -- useful for trip planning.
- **Rate only what you've tried in person.** The rating scale is about the specific item at that specific place, not the restaurant overall.
